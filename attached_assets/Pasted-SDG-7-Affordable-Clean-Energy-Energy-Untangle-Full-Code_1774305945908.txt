SDG 7 – Affordable & Clean Energy: Energy Untangle – Full Coder-Ready Design

Genre: Puzzle / Logic Simulation
Target Audience: Teens, casual players
Tone: Relaxing, strategic, visually satisfying

⸻

1. Core Gameplay Loop
	1.	Display an energy grid connecting power sources (solar, wind, hydro) to buildings.
	2.	Identify tangled, inefficient, or incomplete connections.
	3.	Player untangles and reconnects energy sources to buildings for maximum efficiency.
	4.	Monitor energy flow in real-time.
	5.	Complete each level/puzzle before time, cable, or resource limits are exceeded.
	6.	Progress to increasingly complex grids.

⸻

2. Step-by-Step Player Actions

Step 0: Start Level
	•	Show a visual grid of nodes (energy sources) and buildings needing power.
	•	Highlight tangled or broken connections.
	•	Display metrics: current energy efficiency, number of powered buildings, cable usage.

Step 1: Select Energy Node
	•	Player clicks a solar panel, wind turbine, or hydro node.
	•	Node shows possible connections to buildings or other nodes.

Step 2: Connect or Reconfigure Cables
	•	Drag cables to link nodes to buildings.
	•	Rules:
	•	Each building requires a specific energy type or combination.
	•	Overlapping or incorrect cables reduce efficiency.
	•	Real-time feedback shows energy flow and efficiency.

Step 3: Test Grid
	•	Activate grid: energy flows through cables.
	•	Efficiency, coverage, and losses are calculated dynamically.
	•	Player adjusts connections to maximize efficiency.

Step 4: Monitor Building Power
	•	Buildings indicate power status: fully powered (green), partially powered (yellow), unpowered (red).
	•	Energy efficiency calculated as percentage of total potential energy delivered.

Step 5: End Level
	•	Score calculated: efficiency %, time taken, minimal cable use.
	•	Visual feedback: all buildings powered, smooth energy flow animations.
	•	Unlock next level with additional nodes, buildings, and challenges.

⸻

3. Key Variables & Objects

Energy Node Attributes
	•	Type: solar, wind, hydro
	•	Energy output per unit time
	•	Connection status

Cable Attributes
	•	Start node and end node
	•	Transmission efficiency
	•	Penalty for overlaps or misconnections

Building Attributes
	•	Energy requirement (type or combination)
	•	Power status (0–100%)
	•	Connected nodes list

Global Variables
	•	Grid efficiency percentage
	•	Total energy delivered
	•	Cable usage count
	•	Level number / complexity

⸻

4. UI Elements
	•	Top bar: level, energy efficiency, powered buildings, cable usage, timer (optional)
	•	Grid view: nodes, cables, buildings, real-time energy flow
	•	Node panel: select node, view output, connection info
	•	Alerts: buildings unpowered, energy loss, cable misconnection
	•	End-of-level report: energy efficiency, cable usage, score

⸻

5. Feedback Systems
	•	Visual: buildings light up when powered, energy flows along cables, tangled cables glow red
	•	Audio: connection sounds, energy flow humming, alert sounds
	•	Efficiency meter updates dynamically
	•	Completion animations and score feedback

⸻

6. Progression
	•	Unlock additional energy types: advanced solar panels, offshore wind turbines, pumped hydro
	•	Introduce larger grids with multiple nodes and buildings
	•	Add constraints: limited cable length, fluctuating energy output, time limits
	•	Optional challenges: maximize efficiency while minimizing cable use or losses

⸻

7. Win/Loss Conditions
	•	Win: All buildings fully powered, energy efficiency above threshold (e.g., 90%)
	•	Loss: Buildings unpowered, efficiency falls below threshold, or resources/time exceeded
