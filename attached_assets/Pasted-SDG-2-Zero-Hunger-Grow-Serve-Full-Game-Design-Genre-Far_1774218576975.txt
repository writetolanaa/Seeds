SDG 2 – Zero Hunger: Grow & Serve – Full Game Design

Genre: Farming + Trading Simulation
Target Audience: Kids, teens, casual players
Tone: Vibrant, playful, satisfying

⸻

1. Core Gameplay Loop
	1.	Plant crops on available plots.
	2.	Water and fertilize crops to maintain quality.
	3.	Harvest mature crops.
	4.	Sell or distribute crops to citizens based on demand and preferences.
	5.	Track metrics: hunger levels, citizen happiness, food waste.
	6.	Repeat each in-game day/week.

⸻

2. Step-by-Step Player Actions

Step 0: Start of Day
	•	Display inventory: seeds, water, fertilizer.
	•	Display citizen demand: crop type, quantity, and budget.
	•	Highlight plots ready for planting or harvest.

Step 1: Plant Crops
	•	Player selects crop type and plots for planting.
	•	Assign water and fertilizer levels for each plot.
	•	Growth quality depends on soil quality, water, and fertilizer.

Step 2: Monitor Crop Growth
	•	Crops pass through growth stages: seed → sprout → mature.
	•	Player may intervene for pests, weeds, or weather events.
	•	Crop health and growth progress are updated every in-game hour/day.

Step 3: Harvest
	•	Player clicks on mature crops to harvest.
	•	Harvest yield is calculated using base yield × growth health × fertilizer bonus.
	•	Add harvested crops to inventory.

Step 4: Sell or Distribute to Citizens
	•	Citizens appear with individual preferences and budgets.
	•	Player assigns crops to each citizen.
	•	Satisfaction depends on matching crop type, quantity, and quality to citizen requests.
	•	Metrics updated: citizen hunger reduced, money earned, food waste generated.

Step 5: End of Day
	•	Update global stats: total hunger, total waste, profit.
	•	Display visual feedback: citizen happiness, inventory changes, crop growth.
	•	Prepare for next day’s planting and citizen demands.

⸻

3. Key Variables & Objects

Crop Attributes
	•	Type (e.g., carrot, tomato)
	•	Growth stage (seed, sprout, mature)
	•	Health (0–100)
	•	Water level
	•	Fertilizer level
	•	Yield multiplier

Citizen Attributes
	•	Hunger level (0–100)
	•	Crop preference
	•	Budget
	•	Happiness (0–100)

Global Variables
	•	Day count
	•	Total hunger across all citizens
	•	Total waste generated
	•	Inventory (seeds, harvested crops, water, fertilizer)
	•	Market prices

⸻

4. UI Elements
	•	Top bar: day, money, hunger meter, waste meter
	•	Side panel: inventory of seeds, crops, water, fertilizer
	•	Citizen panel: current requests, budgets, and satisfaction
	•	Plot grid: planting, watering, growth, and harvesting visuals
	•	Alerts: pests, low water, or missed citizen requests

⸻

5. Feedback Systems
	•	Citizens smile when hunger is satisfied; frown if needs are unmet.
	•	Visual effects for crop growth: sprouting, ripening, sparkle for high-quality harvests.
	•	Daily summary screen: hunger reduced, money earned, waste produced.
	•	Audio cues: watering, harvesting, selling.

⸻

6. Progression
	•	Unlock new crops, seeds, and fertilizers as days/weeks progress.
	•	Expand plot size to grow more crops.
	•	Introduce rare crops with higher yield or citizen value.
	•	Challenges: pests, droughts, market fluctuations affecting citizen demand and budgets.

⸻

7. Win/Loss Conditions
	•	Win: All citizens’ hunger levels remain below the critical threshold for a set number of days/weeks.
	•	Loss: Hunger exceeds critical levels or food waste becomes unsustainable.
