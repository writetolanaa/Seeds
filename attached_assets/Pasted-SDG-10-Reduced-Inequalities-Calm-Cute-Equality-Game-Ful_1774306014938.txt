SDG 10 – Reduced Inequalities: Calm & Cute Equality Game – Full Coder-Ready Design

Genre: Casual / Relaxing Simulation
Target Audience: Teens, casual players, all ages
Tone: Calm, cute, educational, low-pressure

⸻

1. Core Gameplay Loop
	1.	Player interacts with a small community of characters with different backgrounds, abilities, and needs.
	2.	Identify inequalities in access to resources, opportunities, and services.
	3.	Take actions to redistribute resources, improve access, and foster inclusion.
	4.	Observe positive changes in character well-being and social cohesion.
	5.	Track equality metrics across the community.
	6.	Repeat daily or session cycles to maintain a harmonious, equitable environment.

⸻

2. Step-by-Step Player Actions

Step 0: Start Day/Session
	•	Display community dashboard: characters, resources, and equality metrics.
	•	Highlight characters or areas with low access or high need.

Step 1: Observe Inequalities
	•	Click on characters or locations to see:
	•	Resource availability (food, toys, tools, access to education/work)
	•	Happiness and well-being levels
	•	Opportunities for growth or development

Step 2: Take Actions
	•	Player redistributes resources: food, books, toys, or tools.
	•	Provide access to services: medical check-ups, education, skill training.
	•	Foster inclusion through social events, mentorship, or collaboration tasks.

Step 3: Monitor Impact
	•	Characters react visibly: smiling, playing, learning, or collaborating.
	•	Metrics update: equality index (0–100), character happiness, social cohesion.
	•	Positive actions increase overall harmony; neglected needs decrease metrics.

Step 4: End of Day/Session
	•	Update metrics for resource usage, equality index, and character satisfaction.
	•	Provide visual feedback: happy, active community; colorful, balanced environment.
	•	Prepare next session with new needs or events.

⸻

3. Key Variables & Objects

Character Attributes
	•	Name, background, and abilities
	•	Resource access (0–100%)
	•	Happiness (0–100)
	•	Participation in community activities

Resource Attributes
	•	Type: food, toys, educational tools, healthcare access
	•	Quantity
	•	Distribution history

Community Metrics
	•	Equality index (0–100)
	•	Average happiness
	•	Social cohesion (0–100)

Global Variables
	•	Day/session count
	•	Total resources available
	•	Events queue (e.g., seasonal needs, emergencies)

⸻

4. UI Elements
	•	Top bar: day/session, equality index, community happiness, available resources
	•	Character panel: list of community members, resource access, happiness
	•	Resource panel: available items, distribution options
	•	Community view: interactive map of homes, schools, playgrounds, workplaces
	•	Alerts: unmet needs, resource shortages, or opportunities for improvement
	•	End-of-day report: equality trends, resource usage, character happiness

⸻

5. Feedback Systems
	•	Visual: characters react positively when needs are met, icons for happiness
	•	Audio: soft chimes for successful actions, gentle alerts for issues
	•	Dynamic metrics: equality index and community happiness bar updates in real-time
	•	Visual harmony: balanced colors and activity in the community map

⸻

6. Progression
	•	Unlock new resources, services, and community programs
	•	Introduce larger communities with more characters and diverse needs
	•	Seasonal or event-based challenges: emergencies, festivals, or new opportunities
	•	Track cumulative equality metrics for long-term community harmony

⸻

7. Win/Loss Conditions
	•	Win: Equality index stays high, character happiness is maintained, and social cohesion remains strong over multiple sessions
	•	Loss: Inequalities persist, resources mismanaged, or happiness and cohesion fall below thresholds
