SDG 6 – Clean Water & Sanitation: Water Stories – Full Game Design

Genre: Story-driven Environmental Simulation / Puzzle
Target Audience: Teens, casual players
Tone: Engaging, educational, story-focused, visual

⸻

1. Core Gameplay Loop
	1.	Track water sources from mountains to cities.
	2.	Detect and clean pollutants (oil spills, waste, chemicals).
	3.	Use tools and strategies to improve water quality.
	4.	Monitor the impact on communities and ecosystems.
	5.	Respond to events like contamination, droughts, or industrial spills.
	6.	Repeat across multiple river segments and story chapters.

⸻

2. Step-by-Step Player Actions

Step 0: Start of Scenario
	•	Player sees the river map with upstream, midstream, and downstream zones.
	•	Water quality indicators: pollution levels, flow rate, ecosystem health.
	•	Citizen needs and ecosystem alerts are visible.

Step 1: Detect Pollution
	•	Identify sources of pollution: oil spills, industrial waste, trash.
	•	Player can scan segments or receive alerts from NPCs.
	•	Pollution has types and severity affecting cleaning strategy.

Step 2: Deploy Cleaning Tools
	•	Tools include absorbent pads, filtration devices, chemical neutralizers, trash collection units.
	•	Player selects tools and applies them to river segments.
	•	Tool effectiveness depends on pollution type, quantity, and river conditions.

Step 3: Manage Resources
	•	Limited cleaning tools and manpower require strategic deployment.
	•	Overuse or misplacement wastes resources and reduces effectiveness.
	•	Resources are replenished gradually or via mission objectives.

Step 4: Monitor Downstream Effects
	•	Water quality improvements upstream affect downstream villages and ecosystems.
	•	NPC health, biodiversity, and river usability update dynamically.
	•	Feedback guides next interventions.

Step 5: End of Scenario / Day
	•	Update metrics: water quality per segment, citizen health, biodiversity health.
	•	Provide visual feedback: cleaner water, thriving wildlife, happy citizens.
	•	Prepare next scenario: new pollution events or upstream challenges.

⸻

3. Key Variables & Objects

River Segment Attributes
	•	Location (upstream, midstream, downstream)
	•	Pollution level (0–100)
	•	Pollution type (oil, trash, chemicals)
	•	Flow rate

NPC Attributes
	•	Health (0–100)
	•	Water access level (0–100)
	•	Satisfaction / trust in player interventions

Tool Attributes
	•	Type (absorbent pad, filter, neutralizer, trash collector)
	•	Efficiency per pollution type
	•	Availability / inventory

Global Variables
	•	Scenario / day count
	•	Overall water quality index
	•	Biodiversity health
	•	Resource inventory

⸻

4. UI Elements
	•	Top bar: day/scenario, overall water quality index, resource inventory
	•	River map: shows pollution levels, flow, and segment status
	•	NPC panel: citizen health, water access, alerts
	•	Tool panel: available cleaning tools, effectiveness indicators
	•	Alerts: new pollution events, downstream problems, resource depletion
	•	End-of-day report: water quality, ecosystem recovery, citizen satisfaction

⸻

5. Feedback Systems
	•	Visual: water clarity, river color, fish and wildlife animation
	•	Audio: water flowing, pollution detection alerts, tool usage sounds
	•	NPCs react to water quality: happy when clean, distressed when polluted
	•	Metrics update dynamically: pollution reduced, ecosystem recovery visible

⸻

6. Progression
	•	Unlock advanced cleaning tools and river monitoring devices as scenarios progress
	•	Introduce larger rivers with multiple pollution sources
	•	Challenge players with natural events: floods, droughts, unexpected industrial spills
	•	Story chapters introduce social and environmental consequences

⸻

7. Win/Loss Conditions
	•	Win: All river segments meet safe water quality thresholds; citizens have full access to clean water; biodiversity restored
	•	Loss: Water quality remains poor downstream, citizens fall ill, pollution persists beyond acceptable levels
