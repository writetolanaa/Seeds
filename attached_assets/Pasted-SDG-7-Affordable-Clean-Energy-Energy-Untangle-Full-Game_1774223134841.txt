SDG 7 – Affordable & Clean Energy: Energy Untangle – Full Game Design

Genre: Puzzle / Logic Simulation
Target Audience: Teens, casual players
Tone: Relaxing, strategic, visually satisfying

⸻

1. Core Gameplay Loop
	1.	Display a tangled energy grid connecting solar panels, wind turbines, and hydro stations to buildings.
	2.	Player untangles and reconnects energy sources to maximize efficiency.
	3.	Monitor energy flow and prevent losses or overloads.
	4.	Track building power status and grid efficiency.
	5.	Complete puzzle before time/resource limits expire.
	6.	Repeat with increasing grid complexity.

⸻

2. Step-by-Step Player Actions

Step 0: Start Level
	•	Display the energy grid: nodes (power sources), cables, and buildings needing power.
	•	Show metrics: energy efficiency, losses, building power status.
	•	Highlight tangled or inefficient connections.

Step 1: Select Energy Node
	•	Player clicks on a solar, wind, or hydro node.
	•	Node displays possible connection paths to buildings or other nodes.

Step 2: Connect or Reconfigure Cables
	•	Player drags and connects cables to target buildings or nodes.
	•	Connection rules:
	•	Each building requires a specific energy type or combination
	•	Overlapping or incorrect cables cause energy loss
	•	Real-time feedback shows current energy flow and efficiency

Step 3: Test Grid
	•	After configuration, energy flows through the network.
	•	Efficiency, power coverage, and energy losses are calculated dynamically.
	•	Player may need to adjust connections to optimize the grid.

Step 4: Monitor Building Power
	•	Buildings light up when fully powered; partially powered buildings show warning.
	•	If a building receives no power, alerts are generated.
	•	Energy efficiency is calculated as a percentage of total potential energy delivered.

Step 5: End of Level
	•	Calculate score: energy efficiency, time taken, minimal cable usage.
	•	Provide visual feedback: fully lit buildings, smooth energy flow animations.
	•	Unlock next level with more complex grids and additional energy sources.

⸻

3. Key Variables & Objects

Energy Node Attributes
	•	Type: solar, wind, hydro
	•	Energy output (per unit time)
	•	Connection status (connected/disconnected)

Cable Attributes
	•	Start and end nodes
	•	Energy transmission efficiency
	•	Overlap penalty (if cables cross incorrectly)

Building Attributes
	•	Required energy type(s)
	•	Power status (0–100%)
	•	Connected nodes list

Global Variables
	•	Grid efficiency percentage
	•	Total energy delivered
	•	Cable usage count
	•	Level number / complexity

⸻

4. UI Elements
	•	Top bar: current level, energy efficiency, score, timer (optional)
	•	Grid view: nodes, cables, buildings, real-time energy flow
	•	Node panel: energy source selection, output information
	•	Alerts: buildings without power, energy loss, misconfigured cables
	•	End-of-level report: energy efficiency, cable usage, score

⸻

5. Feedback Systems
	•	Visual: buildings light up when powered, energy flows along cables, tangled cables glow red
	•	Audio: connection sounds, energy flow humming, alert sounds for failures
	•	Efficiency meter updates dynamically
	•	Score animations and level completion visual effects

⸻

6. Progression
	•	Unlock additional energy types and larger grids with more buildings
	•	Introduce constraints: limited cable length, time limits, or fluctuating energy outputs
	•	Increase complexity: overlapping grids, multiple simultaneous energy sources
	•	Optional challenges: maximize efficiency while minimizing cable use or energy loss

⸻

7. Win/Loss Conditions
	•	Win: All buildings are fully powered and energy efficiency exceeds threshold (e.g., 90%)
	•	Loss: One or more buildings remain unpowered, efficiency falls below threshold, or resource limits exceeded
