SDG 5 – Gender Equality: Switch the Shoes – Full Game Design

Genre: Narrative / Role-Simulation
Target Audience: Teens, young adults
Tone: Fun, creative, thought-provoking, interactive

⸻

1. Core Gameplay Loop
	1.	Player selects a character with a specific gender (male, female, non-binary).
	2.	Navigate daily life scenarios: school, work, public spaces, social interactions.
	3.	Identify and respond to biased situations or barriers.
	4.	Make systemic changes: policies, rules, and environmental adjustments.
	5.	Track equality metrics and citizen satisfaction.
	6.	Repeat across multiple days/scenarios to measure long-term impact.

⸻

2. Step-by-Step Player Actions

Step 0: Character Selection
	•	Player chooses a gender for their avatar.
	•	Each gender experiences different systemic biases and reactions in the world.

Step 1: Navigate Daily Scenarios
	•	Player moves through locations (school, workplace, public spaces).
	•	Encounters challenges: unequal pay, unfair rules, social stereotypes.
	•	Player can choose responses or attempt creative solutions.

Step 2: Identify Inequities
	•	Highlight scenarios where bias affects opportunity or treatment.
	•	Examples: denied promotion, lack of access, different societal expectations.

Step 3: Implement Changes
	•	Apply solutions:
	•	Advocate for policy changes
	•	Modify environmental systems (e.g., workplace rules, school protocols)
	•	Educate NPCs through interactions
	•	Actions have delayed effects on equality metrics and NPC behavior.

Step 4: Track Metrics
	•	After each scenario, player sees:
	•	Equality Index (overall fairness score)
	•	Opportunity distribution across genders
	•	NPC satisfaction and happiness

Step 5: End of Day / Scenario
	•	Visual feedback: citizens react positively or negatively to changes
	•	Update world state → modified rules and systems persist in next day/scenario

⸻

3. Key Variables & Objects

Player Attributes
	•	Gender
	•	Skill in advocacy / problem-solving
	•	Reputation or influence in the world

NPC Attributes
	•	Gender, age, role (student, worker, official)
	•	Happiness / satisfaction
	•	Access to opportunities (education, jobs, resources)

Scenario Attributes
	•	Location type (school, workplace, public space)
	•	Bias events list (predefined inequalities)
	•	Policy/system parameters affecting NPC outcomes

Global Variables
	•	Day / scenario count
	•	Equality Index (0–100)
	•	Gender-specific opportunity metrics
	•	NPC satisfaction averages

⸻

4. UI Elements
	•	Top bar: day/scenario, Equality Index, NPC satisfaction
	•	Scenario panel: current location, pending bias events, possible interventions
	•	Character panel: gender, influence/reputation score, skills
	•	Alerts: bias detected, NPC complaint, successful intervention
	•	End-of-day report: equality progress, NPC reactions, opportunity distribution

⸻

5. Feedback Systems
	•	NPC animations: happy when fairness improves, frustrated when ignored
	•	Visual indicators in locations for bias or fairness levels
	•	Alerts for unresolved inequities or successful interventions
	•	Equality Index bar updates in real-time

⸻

6. Progression
	•	Unlock new locations and more complex scenarios as player improves advocacy skill
	•	Gain tools for systemic change: laws, policies, educational campaigns
	•	Introduce unexpected challenges: societal pushback, stubborn NPC behaviors
	•	Metrics carry over, so long-term planning affects overall world equality

⸻

7. Win/Loss Conditions
	•	Win: Equality Index reaches maximum, opportunities are fairly distributed, and NPC satisfaction is high across all genders
	•	Loss: Persistent inequality, low Equality Index, or high dissatisfaction among NPCs leads to systemic failure in scenarios
