SDG 3 – Good Health & Well-Being: Oops! Hospital! – Full Game Design

Genre: Management / Comedy Simulation
Target Audience: Teens, casual players
Tone: Light-hearted, humorous, educational

⸻

1. Core Gameplay Loop
	1.	Patients arrive with health issues caused by lifestyle habits.
	2.	Player diagnoses problems and assigns interventions (treatment, lifestyle changes, preventative measures).
	3.	Manage hospital resources: staff, beds, medicine, and wellness programs.
	4.	Track population health metrics: patient recovery, overall wellness, and hospital reputation.
	5.	Repeat each in-game day.

⸻

2. Step-by-Step Player Actions

Step 0: Start of Day
	•	Display hospital dashboard: bed availability, staff on duty, medicine stock, and patient queue.
	•	Show population health metrics: overall wellness, disease outbreak risk, stress levels.

Step 1: Admit Patients
	•	Patients arrive with random or seasonal health issues: overeating, lack of sleep, stress, minor injuries, flu.
	•	Each patient has a “lifestyle health score” affecting recovery difficulty.

Step 2: Diagnose & Assign Interventions
	•	Player selects interventions:
	•	Diet plan, exercise program, counseling, medicine, rest.
	•	Each intervention modifies patient health variables over time.
	•	Some interventions require hospital resources (staff time, medicine stock).

Step 3: Monitor Recovery
	•	Track patient recovery progress hourly or daily: health score increases based on assigned treatments and lifestyle changes.
	•	Unexpected events can occur: stress spikes, hospital overcrowding, contagious disease.
	•	Player may need to reassign staff or adjust treatment plans.

Step 4: Preventive Programs
	•	Players can implement hospital-wide wellness initiatives: exercise classes, vaccination campaigns, public health education.
	•	These reduce future patient influx or improve recovery speed.

Step 5: End of Day
	•	Update metrics: patient recovery, hospital reputation, resource levels.
	•	Provide visual feedback: happy patients, overcrowding alerts, staff fatigue indicators.
	•	Prepare next day: new patient queue, resource adjustments, disease trends.

⸻

3. Key Variables & Objects

Patient Attributes
	•	Name, age
	•	Health score (0–100)
	•	Disease type / lifestyle issue
	•	Recovery rate modifier (affected by interventions and hospital efficiency)

Hospital Attributes
	•	Staff availability (doctors, nurses, counselors)
	•	Bed count
	•	Medicine and supplies inventory
	•	Wellness program status

Global Variables
	•	Day count
	•	Population health index
	•	Hospital reputation score
	•	Patient queue length

⸻

4. UI Elements
	•	Top bar: day count, hospital reputation, staff availability, medicine stock
	•	Patient panel: list of patients, health status, required intervention
	•	Resource panel: available staff, beds, medicine, wellness program options
	•	Alerts: patient critical condition, overcrowding, medicine shortage
	•	Daily report: recovery rates, population wellness trends, hospital feedback

⸻

5. Feedback Systems
	•	Patient animations: smiling when recovering, worried when neglected
	•	Visual indicators of overcrowding or high stress
	•	Daily report with stats: recovered patients, hospital reputation, resource consumption
	•	Audio cues: treatment success, emergency alerts, staff activity

⸻

6. Progression
	•	Unlock new treatments and wellness programs as reputation improves
	•	Hire more staff or expand hospital facilities
	•	Introduce seasonal challenges: flu season, heatwaves, stress spikes
	•	Track long-term population health trends to inform strategic decisions

⸻

7. Win/Loss Conditions
	•	Win: Population health index remains high, hospital reputation reaches maximum, and emergency response capacity is maintained over X days.
	•	Loss: Population health drops below critical threshold, hospital becomes overcrowded, or resources are depleted, causing widespread failure to treat patients.
