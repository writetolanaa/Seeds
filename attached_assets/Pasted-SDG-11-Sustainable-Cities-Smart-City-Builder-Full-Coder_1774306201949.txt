SDG 11 – Sustainable Cities: Smart City Builder – Full Coder-Ready Design

Genre: City-Building / Simulation / Strategy
Target Audience: Teens, casual players
Tone: Strategic, creative, educational

⸻

1. Core Gameplay Loop
	1.	Player manages a city with multiple districts and infrastructure systems.
	2.	Build and upgrade sustainable buildings, transport, and public services.
	3.	Ensure accessibility, safety, and environmental sustainability.
	4.	Respond to city events: traffic congestion, pollution spikes, emergencies.
	5.	Track city metrics: citizen satisfaction, pollution, safety, and accessibility.
	6.	Repeat daily, weekly, or seasonal cycles to grow a balanced, sustainable city.

⸻

2. Step-by-Step Player Actions

Step 0: Start Session
	•	Display city dashboard: population, budget, pollution, safety, citizen satisfaction.
	•	Highlight districts needing attention: traffic, low services, or high pollution.

Step 1: Build/Upgrade Infrastructure
	•	Construct buildings: residential, commercial, industrial, public services.
	•	Upgrade systems: energy-efficient buildings, green transport, water management.
	•	Placement affects accessibility, pollution, and citizen happiness.

Step 2: Manage Transportation
	•	Build roads, public transit, bike lanes, and pedestrian paths.
	•	Optimize traffic flow to reduce congestion and emissions.
	•	Adjust public transport schedules and routes.

Step 3: Implement Sustainability Measures
	•	Green spaces: parks, urban forests, green roofs
	•	Renewable energy: solar panels, wind turbines, hydro stations
	•	Waste management: recycling centers, composting, pollution control

Step 4: Respond to Events
	•	Random events: natural disasters, accidents, traffic jams, pollution spikes
	•	Player responds: deploy emergency services, adjust traffic routes, implement cleanup

Step 5: Monitor Metrics
	•	Real-time feedback: citizen happiness, pollution levels, traffic congestion, safety
	•	Adjust city planning and policies based on metrics

Step 6: End of Day/Week
	•	Update metrics: population satisfaction, pollution reduction, safety improvements
	•	Unlock new buildings, districts, or technologies as the city grows

⸻

3. Key Variables & Objects

City Zone Attributes
	•	Type: residential, commercial, industrial, public
	•	Population density
	•	Pollution level (0–100)
	•	Safety index (0–100)

Building Attributes
	•	Type: energy-efficient, standard, public service
	•	Capacity and occupancy
	•	Environmental impact

Transport System Attributes
	•	Roads, public transit, bike lanes, pedestrian paths
	•	Traffic flow efficiency
	•	Emissions impact

Global Variables
	•	Population count
	•	Budget
	•	Citizen satisfaction (0–100)
	•	Pollution level (0–100)
	•	Safety index (0–100)

⸻

4. UI Elements
	•	Top bar: budget, citizen satisfaction, pollution, safety, population
	•	City map: districts, buildings, transport lines, real-time traffic
	•	Building panel: construct, upgrade, demolish options
	•	Event panel: alerts for accidents, disasters, traffic issues
	•	Metrics panel: pollution, safety, population happiness trends
	•	End-of-day/week report: population growth, satisfaction, environmental impact

⸻

5. Feedback Systems
	•	Visual: buildings, parks, and traffic animated; pollution or accidents highlighted
	•	Audio: city ambiance, alerts for events, positive chimes for improvements
	•	Real-time metrics update dynamically: satisfaction, pollution, safety
	•	Citizen reactions: happy, neutral, or unhappy icons based on services

⸻

6. Progression
	•	Unlock advanced sustainable buildings, transport systems, and renewable energy options
	•	Introduce larger, more complex districts with diverse needs
	•	Seasonal challenges: storms, festivals, energy demands
	•	Track cumulative metrics for reputation and unlock rewards

⸻

7. Win/Loss Conditions
	•	Win: Citizen satisfaction is high, pollution and accidents minimized, city is accessible and sustainable across multiple cycles
	•	Loss: Citizen satisfaction drops critically, pollution reaches dangerous levels, or disasters overwhelm city systems
