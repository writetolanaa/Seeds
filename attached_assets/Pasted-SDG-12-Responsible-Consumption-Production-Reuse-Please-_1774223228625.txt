SDG 12 – Responsible Consumption & Production: Reuse Please! – Full Game Design

Genre: Crafting / Sorting / Resource Management
Target Audience: Teens, casual players
Tone: Fun, educational, creative, satisfying

⸻

1. Core Gameplay Loop
	1.	Receive incoming waste items from homes, factories, or stores.
	2.	Sort items into categories: recyclable, reusable, compostable, hazardous.
	3.	Craft new products from reusable materials.
	4.	Track environmental impact: waste reduction, material efficiency, and product value.
	5.	Respond to events: contamination, shortages, or overproduction.
	6.	Repeat daily or by batch cycles.

⸻

2. Step-by-Step Player Actions

Step 0: Start of Day
	•	Display inventory: materials collected, sorting bins, crafting tools.
	•	Show incoming waste batch with item types and quantities.
	•	Display environmental metrics: waste levels, recycling efficiency, production score.

Step 1: Sort Waste
	•	Player drags items into correct bins:
	•	Recyclable → metals, plastics, glass
	•	Reusable → furniture, textiles, electronics
	•	Compostable → organic waste
	•	Hazardous → chemicals, batteries
	•	Incorrect sorting reduces efficiency score and may create penalties.

Step 2: Process Materials
	•	Convert sorted items into reusable raw materials via processing stations: shredders, cleaners, smelters.
	•	Processing takes time or energy resources.

Step 3: Craft Products
	•	Use processed materials to craft new items: furniture, toys, tools, or building materials.
	•	Player selects recipes based on available materials.
	•	Product quality depends on material type and processing efficiency.

Step 4: Manage Inventory & Distribution
	•	Add crafted products to inventory for sale, donation, or deployment in scenarios.
	•	Track product value and contribution to environmental metrics.

Step 5: End of Day / Batch
	•	Update metrics: total waste reduced, materials reused, products created, efficiency rating.
	•	Provide visual and numerical feedback: sorted bins, crafted items, environmental score.

⸻

3. Key Variables & Objects

Item Attributes
	•	Type: recyclable, reusable, compostable, hazardous
	•	Quality: low, medium, high (affects final product value)
	•	Quantity

Material Attributes
	•	Type: metal, plastic, wood, cloth, organic
	•	Quantity
	•	Purity / processing quality

Product Attributes
	•	Type: furniture, toy, tool, building material
	•	Quality score (based on raw materials)
	•	Inventory count

Global Variables
	•	Day count
	•	Total waste received, sorted, and reused
	•	Efficiency score (percentage of waste properly reused)
	•	Inventory of materials and crafted products

⸻

4. UI Elements
	•	Top bar: day, environmental efficiency score, inventory count
	•	Waste batch panel: incoming items, type, quantity
	•	Sorting bins panel: visual feedback for correct/incorrect sorting
	•	Processing stations panel: tools and processing progress
	•	Crafted products panel: inventory, value, and quality
	•	Alerts: contamination, shortage, or mis-sorted items

⸻

5. Feedback Systems
	•	Visual: bins fill up, materials move along processing stations, crafted products appear in inventory
	•	Audio: sorting sounds, processing machine sounds, product completion chime
	•	Efficiency meter updates dynamically
	•	Daily report: waste reduced, materials reused, products crafted, efficiency score

⸻

6. Progression
	•	Unlock advanced processing machines and crafting recipes
	•	Introduce larger batches and more complex waste types
	•	Optional challenges: reduce hazardous waste, maximize reuse efficiency, or craft high-value items
	•	Track cumulative environmental impact for long-term progression

⸻

7. Win/Loss Conditions
	•	Win: Waste efficiency reaches a set threshold, material reuse maximized, and high-value products crafted consistently
	•	Loss: Waste accumulates beyond acceptable limits, efficiency drops critically, or crafting stalls due to mismanagement
