SDG 17 – Partnerships for the Goals: Global Link

Genre: Strategy / Diplomacy / Resource Coordination
Target Audience: Teens, young adults
Tone: Strategic, cooperative, hopeful, global

⸻

1. Core Game Idea

The player acts as a global coordinator whose job is to connect countries, organizations, NGOs, companies, and communities so that all other SDGs can succeed.

The game is about cooperation, not competition.
No single actor can win alone.

⸻

2. Core Gameplay Loop
	1.	Identify global problems and development gaps.
	2.	Build partnerships between actors with complementary strengths.
	3.	Negotiate agreements (funding, knowledge, technology, logistics).
	4.	Coordinate joint projects across regions.
	5.	Track partnership effectiveness and global progress.
	6.	Repeat across global cycles (years/summits).

⸻

3. Step-by-Step Player Actions

Step 0: Start of Global Cycle
	•	Player sees the world dashboard:
	•	Global cooperation index
	•	Funding availability
	•	Knowledge-sharing level
	•	Technology access
	•	Active global challenges appear (climate, health, education, infrastructure).

⸻

Step 1: Analyze Needs
	•	Player clicks a region or country to view:
	•	Which SDGs are struggling
	•	Available resources
	•	Missing capabilities (money, expertise, tech, logistics)

Each region has strengths and weaknesses.

⸻

Step 2: Select Partners
	•	Player chooses multiple actors:
	•	Governments
	•	NGOs
	•	Universities
	•	Private companies
	•	Local communities

Each actor has:
	•	Strengths (funding, tech, labor, data)
	•	Limits (budget, politics, geography)

⸻

Step 3: Negotiate Agreements
	•	Player sets partnership terms:
	•	Who provides funding
	•	Who shares technology
	•	Who manages implementation
	•	How benefits are distributed

Poor negotiation leads to:
	•	Distrust
	•	Withdrawn partners
	•	Failed projects

Fair agreements increase long-term cooperation.

⸻

Step 4: Launch Joint Projects

Projects may support:
	•	Clean energy
	•	Education systems
	•	Healthcare access
	•	Infrastructure
	•	Climate resilience

Projects run over multiple turns and require:
	•	Continuous cooperation
	•	Resource flow
	•	Political stability

⸻

Step 5: Monitor Cooperation
	•	Track:
	•	Project progress
	•	Partner satisfaction
	•	Transparency
	•	Impact on SDG metrics

Player can intervene if:
	•	Partners disagree
	•	Resources stall
	•	Trust drops

⸻

Step 6: End of Cycle
	•	Global reports summarize:
	•	Successful partnerships
	•	Failed collaborations
	•	SDG progress
	•	New challenges and partners appear.

⸻

4. Key Game Systems & Variables

Global Metrics
	•	Cooperation Index (0–100)
	•	Trust Level (0–100)
	•	Funding Efficiency (0–100)
	•	Knowledge Exchange Level (0–100)

⸻

Partner Attributes
	•	Type (government, NGO, company, academic)
	•	Resources provided
	•	Reliability
	•	Political or ethical constraints

⸻

Project Variables
	•	Duration
	•	Required resources
	•	Risk level
	•	SDGs impacted
	•	Success probability

⸻

5. Player Abilities

The player can:
	•	Build long-term alliances
	•	Mediate disputes between partners
	•	Increase transparency
	•	Improve data sharing
	•	Create global forums and summits
	•	Encourage technology transfer

Abilities unlock as trust and cooperation grow.

⸻

6. User Interface Design
	•	World map: regions, active projects, partnerships
	•	Partner panel: strengths, satisfaction, commitments
	•	Negotiation panel: sliders for responsibility sharing
	•	Project panel: timelines, progress bars, risks
	•	Global dashboard: cooperation, trust, SDG progress
	•	End-of-cycle report: outcomes and lessons learned

⸻

7. Feedback Systems

Visual
	•	Lines connecting partners across the globe
	•	Projects visually progress on the map
	•	World becomes more vibrant as cooperation improves

Narrative
	•	Global news updates
	•	Partner statements
	•	Summit outcomes

Audio
	•	Calm global music
	•	Alert tones for conflicts
	•	Success sounds for strong partnerships

⸻

8. Progression System
	•	Start with fragmented global cooperation
	•	Unlock:
	•	Multilateral agreements
	•	Global funds
	•	Technology-sharing platforms
	•	Emergency response coalitions
	•	Later stages require managing many SDGs simultaneously

⸻

9. Win / Loss Conditions

Win
	•	High global cooperation
	•	Multiple SDGs improving simultaneously
	•	Long-lasting, fair partnerships
	•	Strong trust between global actors

Loss
	•	Partnerships collapse
	•	Trust erodes
	•	Projects fail repeatedly
	•	Global progress stalls

⸻

10. Educational Outcome

The player learns that:
	•	No SDG can succeed alone
	•	Trust is more valuable than money
	•	Fair partnerships outperform short-term deals
	•	Global problems require shared responsibility
