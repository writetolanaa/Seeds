SDG 16 – Peace, Justice & Strong Institutions: Justice Quest

Genre: Narrative Simulation / Decision-Based Strategy
Target Audience: Teens, young adults
Tone: Serious but accessible, thoughtful, empowering

⸻

1. Core Game Idea

The player manages a small society (town, district, or country) and must build trust, justice, peace, and strong institutions.
The game focuses on fair decision-making, transparency, conflict resolution, and rule of law, not violence.

⸻

2. Core Gameplay Loop
	1.	Observe conflicts, disputes, corruption, or institutional failures.
	2.	Investigate problems using limited information.
	3.	Make decisions through laws, mediation, courts, or reforms.
	4.	See short-term and long-term consequences.
	5.	Track trust, peace, and justice metrics.
	6.	Repeat across days, cases, and story chapters.

⸻

3. Step-by-Step Player Actions

Step 0: Start of Day / Turn
	•	Player sees a dashboard showing:
	•	Public trust
	•	Peace level
	•	Justice fairness score
	•	Institutional strength
	•	New cases or events appear (disputes, corruption reports, protests).

⸻

Step 1: Review a Case or Issue

Cases can include:
	•	A conflict between two groups
	•	A report of corruption
	•	An unfair arrest
	•	A protest against unfair laws
	•	A missing transparency issue

Each case shows:
	•	People involved
	•	Initial evidence
	•	Public opinion (which may be biased or incomplete)

⸻

Step 2: Investigate

Player chooses investigation actions:
	•	Interview witnesses
	•	Request documents
	•	Send inspectors
	•	Hold public hearings

Each action:
	•	Costs time or resources
	•	Reveals partial or full information
	•	May increase or decrease public trust

⸻

Step 3: Make a Decision

Based on gathered information, player chooses an action:
	•	Mediate peacefully between parties
	•	Send the case to court
	•	Reform a rule or law
	•	Punish corruption
	•	Protect human rights
	•	Increase transparency (publish reports)

Decisions can be:
	•	Fast but risky
	•	Slow but fair
	•	Popular but unjust
	•	Unpopular but necessary

⸻

Step 4: Consequences

The game simulates outcomes:
	•	Peace level may rise or fall
	•	Trust in institutions changes
	•	Future cases become easier or harder
	•	Groups may cooperate or radicalize

Consequences are:
	•	Immediate (public reaction)
	•	Long-term (institutional strength)

⸻

Step 5: End of Day / Turn
	•	Metrics update visibly
	•	News headlines summarize the day
	•	Player prepares for the next set of challenges

⸻

4. Key Game Systems & Variables

Society Metrics
	•	Peace Level (0–100)
	•	Public Trust (0–100)
	•	Justice Fairness (0–100)
	•	Institutional Strength (0–100)

Case Variables
	•	Conflict intensity
	•	Evidence completeness
	•	Public bias level
	•	Urgency

Institution Variables
	•	Court efficiency
	•	Transparency level
	•	Corruption risk
	•	Law enforcement fairness

⸻

5. Player Abilities

The player can:
	•	Reform laws
	•	Improve courts
	•	Train officials
	•	Increase transparency systems
	•	Create peaceful dialogue programs
	•	Strengthen anti-corruption measures

Abilities unlock gradually as institutions improve.

⸻

6. User Interface Design
	•	Top bar: peace, trust, justice, institutions
	•	Case panel: active disputes and reports
	•	Investigation panel: actions and costs
	•	Decision panel: available responses
	•	News panel: public reaction and narrative feedback
	•	End-of-turn summary: metric changes and consequences

⸻

7. Feedback Systems

Visual
	•	Calm city when peace is high
	•	Protests or unrest when trust is low
	•	Institutions visually upgrade as they strengthen

Narrative
	•	News headlines
	•	Citizen quotes
	•	Case follow-ups

Audio
	•	Calm ambient sounds during peace
	•	Tense music during crises
	•	Positive chimes for fair resolutions

⸻

8. Progression System
	•	Start with weak institutions and limited authority
	•	Unlock:
	•	Independent courts
	•	Anti-corruption agencies
	•	Transparency tools
	•	Community mediation systems
	•	Later stages introduce complex, multi-layered conflicts

⸻

9. Win / Loss Conditions

Win
	•	Peace remains high
	•	Justice is fair and consistent
	•	Institutions are strong and trusted
	•	Conflicts are resolved without violence

Loss
	•	Trust collapses
	•	Corruption spreads
	•	Justice becomes biased
	•	Society descends into unrest or instability

⸻

10. Educational Outcome

The player learns that:
	•	Peace is built through fairness, not force
	•	Institutions matter more than single decisions
	•	Transparency prevents corruption
	•	Justice requires patience and consistency
