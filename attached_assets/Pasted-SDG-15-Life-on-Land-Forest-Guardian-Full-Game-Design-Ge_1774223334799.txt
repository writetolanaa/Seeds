SDG 15 – Life on Land: Forest Guardian – Full Game Design

Genre: Environmental Simulation / Strategy
Target Audience: Teens, casual players
Tone: Engaging, educational, visually rich

⸻

1. Core Gameplay Loop
	1.	Monitor a forested region for deforestation, wildfires, poaching, and invasive species.
	2.	Plant trees, restore habitats, and manage wildlife populations.
	3.	Track forest health metrics: tree cover, biodiversity, and ecosystem stability.
	4.	Respond to random events like fires, illegal logging, or droughts.
	5.	Repeat across days, seasons, or years to restore and maintain a healthy forest.

⸻

2. Step-by-Step Player Actions

Step 0: Start of Day/Season
	•	Display forest dashboard: tree cover %, biodiversity, wildlife population, ecosystem health.
	•	Highlight urgent issues: fires, logging activity, animal threats, disease outbreaks.

Step 1: Plant & Restore
	•	Player plants trees and restores habitats in degraded areas.
	•	Choose tree type based on local ecosystem needs.
	•	Trees grow over time, improving forest cover and ecosystem stability.

Step 2: Protect Wildlife
	•	Identify endangered species and protect their habitats.
	•	Patrol zones to prevent poaching or rescue injured animals.
	•	Success increases biodiversity index and animal population health.

Step 3: Manage Threats
	•	Respond to wildfires by assigning firefighting teams or creating firebreaks.
	•	Handle invasive species or diseases with targeted interventions.
	•	Prevent illegal logging by placing guards or monitoring activity.

Step 4: Allocate Resources
	•	Limited manpower, tools, and budget require prioritization of planting, protection, and threat management.
	•	Efficient use of resources maximizes forest recovery and biodiversity.

Step 5: End of Day/Season
	•	Update forest metrics: tree cover %, biodiversity, ecosystem health.
	•	Provide visual feedback: greener forest, thriving wildlife, restored habitats.
	•	Prepare next day/season with new events or challenges.

⸻

3. Key Variables & Objects

Forest Zone Attributes
	•	Type: rainforest, temperate forest, savanna, etc.
	•	Tree cover % (0–100)
	•	Biodiversity index (0–100)
	•	Ecosystem health (0–100)
	•	Threats present: fire, poaching, disease, invasive species

Tree Attributes
	•	Species type
	•	Growth stage (seedling → sapling → mature)
	•	Health (0–100)
	•	Ecosystem contribution

Wildlife Attributes
	•	Species type
	•	Population count
	•	Health (0–100)
	•	Habitat area

Global Variables
	•	Day/season/year count
	•	Total resources: manpower, budget, tools
	•	Forest health index
	•	Biodiversity index
	•	Threat status flags

⸻

4. UI Elements
	•	Top bar: day/season/year, forest health index, biodiversity index, resources available
	•	Forest map: zones with visual tree cover, wildlife icons, and threats
	•	Wildlife panel: species health, population, and endangered status
	•	Resource panel: planting tools, firefighting units, monitoring equipment
	•	Alerts: wildfires, poaching, disease outbreaks, invasive species
	•	End-of-day/season report: tree growth, biodiversity changes, ecosystem recovery

⸻

5. Feedback Systems
	•	Visual: trees grow over time, animals move, green canopy expands, threats highlighted
	•	Audio: forest ambiance, alert sounds for threats, planting and wildlife sounds
	•	Metrics: forest health, biodiversity, ecosystem stability update dynamically
	•	Alerts for critical events or poor resource allocation

⸻

6. Progression
	•	Unlock advanced tools: firefighting units, monitoring drones, habitat restoration kits
	•	Introduce larger forest areas and more complex threats
	•	Seasonal challenges: droughts, storms, disease outbreaks
	•	Track cumulative forest health and biodiversity improvements for long-term progression

⸻

7. Win/Loss Conditions
	•	Win: Forest cover, biodiversity, and ecosystem health reach target thresholds over a defined period
	•	Loss: Forest degraded beyond recovery, biodiversity collapse, or resources depleted before threats are controlled
