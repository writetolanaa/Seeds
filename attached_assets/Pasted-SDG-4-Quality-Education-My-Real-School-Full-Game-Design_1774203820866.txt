SDG 4 – Quality Education: My Real School – Full Game Design

Genre: School Simulation / Management
Target Audience: Teens, casual players
Tone: Realistic but friendly, focused on education, inclusion, and student well-being

⸻

1. Core Gameplay Loop
	1.	Assign students to classes and activities.
	2.	Schedule teachers and manage lessons.
	3.	Monitor student academic performance, social skills, and emotional health.
	4.	Respond to events: exams, clubs, conflicts, or burnout.
	5.	Track school-wide metrics: knowledge, happiness, graduation readiness.
	6.	Repeat daily or weekly.

⸻

2. Step-by-Step Player Actions

Step 0: Start of Day
	•	Display school dashboard: student roster, teacher availability, class schedules.
	•	Show key metrics: student happiness, average grades, participation in clubs.

Step 1: Assign Students to Classes
	•	Place students in classrooms based on their learning needs and abilities.
	•	Each classroom has capacity and subject type.
	•	Student learning efficiency depends on teacher skill, class size, and engagement.

Step 2: Assign Teachers
	•	Select teachers for each class.
	•	Teachers have attributes: subject expertise, teaching style, energy level.
	•	Assign teachers to maximize student knowledge gain and engagement.

Step 3: Manage Student Activities
	•	Players schedule extracurricular activities: clubs, sports, art, or volunteering.
	•	Activities improve social skills, happiness, and specific talents.
	•	Track student energy and avoid burnout.

Step 4: Respond to Events
	•	Random or scheduled events: exams, bullying incidents, student stress, parental requests.
	•	Player chooses actions: counseling, schedule changes, classroom interventions.
	•	Student metrics adjust based on response effectiveness.

Step 5: End of Day
	•	Update student knowledge, happiness, social skills, and fatigue.
	•	Visual feedback: happy or tired students, classroom engagement, overall school environment.
	•	Prepare next day’s schedule and teacher assignments.

⸻

3. Key Variables & Objects

Student Attributes
	•	Name, age
	•	Knowledge level (0–100)
	•	Happiness (0–100)
	•	Social skills (0–100)
	•	Energy / fatigue (0–100)
	•	Assigned classes and activities

Teacher Attributes
	•	Name, subject expertise
	•	Teaching skill (0–100)
	•	Energy / stress level
	•	Engagement with students

School Attributes
	•	Classrooms: capacity, subject type
	•	Clubs / activities: participation and impact
	•	Metrics: average knowledge, happiness, graduation readiness

Global Variables
	•	Day count
	•	Academic calendar (exam dates, holidays)
	•	School reputation
	•	Resources (budget for upgrades, activities, and teachers)

⸻

4. UI Elements
	•	Top bar: day, school reputation, budget, overall student happiness and knowledge
	•	Student panel: list of students, individual metrics, class and activity assignments
	•	Teacher panel: list of teachers, skill levels, energy
	•	Classroom view: current lessons, engagement levels
	•	Alerts: exams, conflicts, burnout, special events
	•	Daily summary: academic progress, happiness trends, club performance

⸻

5. Feedback Systems
	•	Student animations: engaged, happy, stressed, or tired
	•	Classroom visual indicators: participation, overcrowding, engagement
	•	Alerts for underperforming or unhappy students
	•	Daily report: knowledge gain, student happiness, club achievements

⸻

6. Progression
	•	Unlock new classrooms, subjects, and extracurricular programs as school reputation grows
	•	Hire additional teachers or specialized staff (counselors, coaches)
	•	Introduce challenges: exams, conflicts, budget limitations, or unexpected events
	•	Track long-term student development and graduation readiness

⸻

7. Win/Loss Conditions
	•	Win: Majority of students graduate with high knowledge, happiness, and social skills; school reputation is maximized
	•	Loss: Students fall below minimum knowledge, happiness drops critically, or school resources are depleted, causing poor outcomes
