SDG 8 – Decent Work & Economic Growth: Company Coach – Full Game Design

Genre: Decision-based Simulation / Management
Target Audience: Teens, adults, casual strategy players
Tone: Strategic, realistic, educational

⸻

1. Core Gameplay Loop
	1.	Review company metrics: productivity, worker satisfaction, finances.
	2.	Assign policies: wages, schedules, training programs, safety measures.
	3.	Implement decisions and simulate their effects on workers and company performance.
	4.	Respond to unexpected events: labor disputes, market fluctuations, accidents.
	5.	Track key performance indicators (KPIs) over time.
	6.	Repeat weekly/monthly cycles, gradually expanding company operations.

⸻

2. Step-by-Step Player Actions

Step 0: Start of Week
	•	Display company dashboard: total employees, budget, worker satisfaction, productivity, safety index.
	•	Highlight urgent issues: complaints, low morale, upcoming deadlines.

Step 1: Assign Policies
	•	Player adjusts:
	•	Wages: increases improve satisfaction but reduce profit
	•	Work schedules: flexible hours improve happiness; overwork reduces productivity
	•	Training programs: improve skills and efficiency but cost resources
	•	Safety measures: prevent accidents; costs money but reduce downtime

Step 2: Allocate Staff Resources
	•	Assign workers to projects, shifts, and roles.
	•	Consider worker skills, fatigue, and morale when assigning tasks.

Step 3: Monitor Events
	•	Random events may occur:
	•	Equipment failure, strikes, sudden workload spikes
	•	Player decides interventions: temporary hiring, overtime adjustments, or policy changes.

Step 4: Simulate Outcomes
	•	Workers complete tasks based on skill level, fatigue, and morale.
	•	Update KPIs: productivity, worker satisfaction, safety incidents, profit.
	•	Visual feedback shows staff mood and project progress.

Step 5: End of Week / Month
	•	Summarize results: profit, productivity, employee happiness, safety incidents.
	•	Adjust company reputation based on overall performance.
	•	Prepare next week: new projects, incoming hires, updated events.

⸻

3. Key Variables & Objects

Worker Attributes
	•	Name, age
	•	Skill level (0–100)
	•	Happiness / satisfaction (0–100)
	•	Fatigue / energy (0–100)
	•	Assigned role or project

Company Attributes
	•	Budget
	•	Productivity score (0–100)
	•	Worker satisfaction average
	•	Safety index (0–100)
	•	Reputation

Policies
	•	Wage levels
	•	Work schedules
	•	Training programs
	•	Safety measures

Global Variables
	•	Week / month count
	•	Total profit
	•	Cumulative KPIs (productivity, satisfaction, safety)

⸻

4. UI Elements
	•	Top bar: week/month, budget, productivity, satisfaction, safety index
	•	Worker panel: list of employees, skills, energy, happiness
	•	Project panel: ongoing tasks, deadlines, worker assignments
	•	Policy panel: adjustable wages, schedules, training, safety
	•	Alerts: accidents, disputes, morale drops, project delays
	•	Summary screen: weekly or monthly KPI overview

⸻

5. Feedback Systems
	•	Worker animations: happy, stressed, tired
	•	Visual indicators for project progress, safety incidents, and morale
	•	Alerts for urgent events or low performance metrics
	•	End-of-period report with graphs showing trends in productivity, satisfaction, safety, and profit

⸻

6. Progression
	•	Unlock new departments, projects, or locations as company grows
	•	Access advanced training programs, improved safety measures, and higher-tier wages
	•	Introduce market challenges, competitor pressures, and economic fluctuations
	•	Track cumulative KPIs to unlock bonuses or reputation rewards

⸻

7. Win/Loss Conditions
	•	Win: Company meets productivity goals, maintains high worker satisfaction, stays profitable, and avoids safety incidents over X weeks/months
	•	Loss: Worker satisfaction drops critically, productivity falls below threshold, repeated safety incidents occur, or company runs out of funds
