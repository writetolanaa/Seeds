SDG 8 – Decent Work & Economic Growth: Company Coach – Full Coder-Ready Design

Genre: Management / Decision Simulation
Target Audience: Teens, adults, casual strategy players
Tone: Strategic, realistic, educational

⸻

1. Core Gameplay Loop
	1.	Review company metrics: employee productivity, satisfaction, finances.
	2.	Assign company policies and manage staff assignments.
	3.	Implement decisions and simulate their impact on workers and company performance.
	4.	Respond to random events: market fluctuations, strikes, accidents, deadlines.
	5.	Track KPIs (productivity, worker satisfaction, profit, safety).
	6.	Repeat weekly or monthly cycles, expanding operations as the company grows.

⸻

2. Step-by-Step Player Actions

Step 0: Start of Week/Month
	•	Display company dashboard: total employees, budget, productivity, satisfaction, safety index.
	•	Highlight urgent issues: complaints, project delays, low morale.

Step 1: Assign Policies
	•	Adjust company policies:
	•	Wages: Increasing boosts morale but reduces profit.
	•	Work schedules: Flexible hours improve happiness; overwork reduces productivity.
	•	Training programs: Improve skills and efficiency but cost resources.
	•	Safety measures: Reduce accidents but cost money.

Step 2: Allocate Staff Resources
	•	Assign workers to projects or shifts based on skill, morale, and energy.
	•	Monitor fatigue and happiness to prevent burnout.

Step 3: Respond to Random Events
	•	Unexpected events occur:
	•	Equipment failure
	•	Labor disputes
	•	Sudden workload spikes
	•	Player decides interventions: temporary hiring, overtime adjustments, policy tweaks.

Step 4: Simulate Outcomes
	•	Workers complete tasks; outcomes depend on skill, fatigue, and morale.
	•	KPIs update dynamically: productivity, satisfaction, safety, profit.
	•	Visual feedback shows staff mood and project progress.

Step 5: End of Week/Month
	•	Present summary: profit, productivity, worker satisfaction, safety incidents.
	•	Update company reputation based on results.
	•	Prepare next week: new projects, incoming hires, updated events.

⸻

3. Key Variables & Objects

Worker Attributes
	•	Name, age
	•	Skill level (0–100)
	•	Happiness / satisfaction (0–100)
	•	Fatigue / energy (0–100)
	•	Assigned role/project

Company Attributes
	•	Budget
	•	Productivity score (0–100)
	•	Worker satisfaction (0–100)
	•	Safety index (0–100)
	•	Reputation

Policies
	•	Wage levels
	•	Work schedules
	•	Training programs
	•	Safety measures

Global Variables
	•	Week/Month count
	•	Total profit
	•	Cumulative KPIs: productivity, satisfaction, safety

⸻

4. UI Elements
	•	Top bar: week/month, budget, productivity, satisfaction, safety index
	•	Worker panel: list of employees, skill, energy, happiness
	•	Project panel: tasks, deadlines, assignments
	•	Policy panel: adjustable wages, schedules, training, safety
	•	Alerts: accidents, disputes, project delays, low morale
	•	End-of-period report: graphs for productivity, satisfaction, safety, profit trends

⸻

5. Feedback Systems
	•	Worker animations: happy, stressed, tired
	•	Visual indicators: project progress, safety alerts, morale changes
	•	Alerts: critical events, low performance metrics
	•	End-of-period report with visual graphs and KPIs

⸻

6. Progression
	•	Unlock new departments, projects, and offices as company grows
	•	Access advanced training, improved safety measures, and wage options
	•	Introduce market pressures, competitor challenges, and economic events
	•	Track cumulative KPIs to unlock bonuses, new hires, or reputation rewards

⸻

7. Win/Loss Conditions
	•	Win: Company achieves high productivity, maintains worker satisfaction, stays profitable, avoids safety incidents over a set period.
	•	Loss: Worker satisfaction falls critically, productivity drops, repeated safety incidents occur, or company runs out of funds.
