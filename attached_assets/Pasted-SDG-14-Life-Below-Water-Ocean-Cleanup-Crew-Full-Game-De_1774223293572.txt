SDG 14 – Life Below Water: Ocean Cleanup Crew – Full Game Design

Genre: Environmental Simulation / Action-Puzzle
Target Audience: Teens, casual players
Tone: Fun, satisfying, educational, visually engaging

⸻

1. Core Gameplay Loop
	1.	Patrol ocean zones for pollution hotspots (plastics, oil, chemical spills).
	2.	Deploy cleanup tools to remove pollutants and restore marine habitats.
	3.	Rescue or protect marine life affected by pollution.
	4.	Track ocean health metrics: water quality, biodiversity, and pollution reduction.
	5.	Respond to environmental events: storms, oil leaks, or illegal dumping.
	6.	Repeat patrols across increasingly challenging zones.

⸻

2. Step-by-Step Player Actions

Step 0: Start of Mission
	•	Display ocean map divided into zones: shallow coast, reef, deep sea.
	•	Show pollution levels and marine life health per zone.
	•	Display available cleanup tools and resources.

Step 1: Detect Pollution
	•	Player scans or navigates zones to locate pollution hotspots.
	•	Pollution types: plastics, oil, chemical waste, ghost nets.
	•	Severity affects how much effort and resources are needed to clean.

Step 2: Deploy Cleanup Tools
	•	Tools include:
	•	Skimmer boats for oil
	•	Nets and grabbers for plastics
	•	Filters for chemical pollutants
	•	Bioremediation kits for long-term cleanup
	•	Player assigns tools to pollution hotspots.
	•	Each tool has efficiency based on pollution type and severity.

Step 3: Rescue or Protect Marine Life
	•	Identify affected species: fish, turtles, coral, marine mammals.
	•	Player performs actions: disentangle animals, relocate endangered species, restore coral reefs.
	•	Success improves biodiversity index and citizen satisfaction metrics.

Step 4: Manage Resources
	•	Limited tools and energy require prioritization.
	•	Overuse reduces effectiveness or breaks tools.
	•	Resources replenish gradually or via mission rewards.

Step 5: End of Mission / Day
	•	Update metrics: cleaned pollution, biodiversity recovered, marine health improved.
	•	Visual feedback: clear water, healthy fish, thriving coral reefs.
	•	Prepare next zone with higher pollution complexity or new hazards.

⸻

3. Key Variables & Objects

Ocean Zone Attributes
	•	Type: coast, reef, deep sea
	•	Pollution level (0–100)
	•	Pollution type: plastics, oil, chemicals, ghost nets
	•	Marine life health (0–100)

Tool Attributes
	•	Type: skimmer, net, filter, bioremediation kit
	•	Efficiency per pollution type
	•	Availability / durability

Marine Life Attributes
	•	Species type
	•	Health (0–100)
	•	Population count
	•	Pollution impact level

Global Variables
	•	Mission / day count
	•	Total pollution removed
	•	Ocean health index (0–100)
	•	Biodiversity index (0–100)
	•	Tool inventory and energy/resources

⸻

4. UI Elements
	•	Top bar: mission/day, ocean health index, biodiversity index, tool inventory
	•	Ocean map: zones with visual pollution indicators
	•	Tool panel: available cleanup tools and efficiency ratings
	•	Marine life panel: species health, population, alerts
	•	Alerts: pollution spikes, tool breakage, endangered species in danger
	•	End-of-day report: pollution removed, biodiversity recovered, ocean health improvements

⸻

5. Feedback Systems
	•	Visual: water clarity improves, fish swim happily, coral colors restored
	•	Audio: splashing, cleanup equipment sounds, alert tones for endangered species
	•	Efficiency meter updates dynamically
	•	Alerts and animations for pollution, tool usage, and wildlife recovery

⸻

6. Progression
	•	Unlock advanced cleanup tools and faster boats
	•	Introduce larger ocean zones with multiple pollution types
	•	Add complex events: oil spills, storms, illegal dumping ships
	•	Track cumulative ocean health and marine biodiversity over multiple missions

⸻

7. Win/Loss Conditions
	•	Win: Ocean health and biodiversity indices reach target thresholds, all pollution hotspots cleaned, and marine life restored
	•	Loss: Pollution persists or increases, marine life dies or endangered species lost, tools depleted before completing cleanup
